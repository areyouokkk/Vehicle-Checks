Tyre Pressure

The tyres are inflated to the correct pressure, as specified by the manufacturer.

Tread Depth

The tread depth is above the minimum legal limit (generally 1.6mm in most regions).

Visible Damage

The tyres are free from cuts, bulges, punctures, or other visible signs of damage.

Tyre Valve Condition

The tyre valves are intact, with no cracks or leaks.

Tyre Wear Pattern

The tyres show even wear across the tread, with no signs of uneven wear that could indicate alignment or suspension issues.

Tyre Age

The tyres are not older than 6-10 years, and the date code on the tyre is within a safe range.

Cracks or Dry Rot

The sidewalls of the tyres are free from cracks, splits, or any signs of dry rot.

Flat Tyres

None of the tyres are flat or significantly under-inflated (visibly low or soft to the touch).

Foreign Objects

There are no foreign objects like nails, stones, or debris stuck in the tread that could cause a puncture.
